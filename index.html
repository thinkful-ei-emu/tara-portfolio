About me

I am an up-and-coming full-stack web developer currently enrolled in the Engineering Immersion program at Thinkful.

In my past endeavors, I’ve always found joy in re-engineering systems and processes to better meet the goals of my organization. From redesigning the student committee system at my college, to planning routines and projects for teenagers as a public school teacher, I’ve always looked deeper to find solutions. Coding gives me the opportunity to incorporate that joy into my daily work.

After I’ve had my fill of functions and for loops, I spend my time playing guitar and writing music, playing board games or video games, lifting weights, or daydreaming about starting a waste-free full-service grocery store here in Houston, Texas. I always try to find time to travel, taking weekend trips to Texas’ various state parks, and, when I can, going a little farther out to see new and exciting places. If I can’t get out of the city, exploring the many incredible restaurants of America's most diverse city, Houston, will do. (Can you tell I totally love h-town?)

Despite now finding coding a constant source of fulfillment in my life, I never really even considered this path a possibility for me, an English teacher with a degree in history and economics. It was at a wedding, talking to someone I hadn’t seen in years, when I first heard about coding bootcamps. I had mentioned my desire to leave teaching and try something new and my passion for systems. He recommended coding school, and I made one of the most exciting choices of my lifetime.

I don’t know where my coding journey will take me. I have some dreams and aspirations—an interactive Dungeons and Dragons character sheet for non-believers, a truly thorough workout planner and tracker. Mainly, I will be happy if I can do good work for a company that makes me proud.
